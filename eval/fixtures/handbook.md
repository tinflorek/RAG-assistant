# Aurora AD-7 Drone — Operator Handbook

## Overview

The Aurora AD-7 is a quad-rotor inspection drone manufactured by Lumen Aerospace.
It ships with firmware version 4.2.1 and is intended for industrial site surveys.

## Battery and Power

The AD-7 uses a removable LX-900 lithium-polymer battery pack rated at 5200 mAh.
A full charge takes 95 minutes using the supplied 65-watt charger. On a single
charge the AD-7 delivers up to 38 minutes of flight time under normal load.

## Flight Envelope

Maximum service altitude is 4500 meters above sea level. The maximum control range
between the AD-7 and its remote is 8 kilometers in open conditions. The drone weighs
1240 grams including the LX-900 battery.

## Operating Conditions

The AD-7 is rated for operation between -10 degrees Celsius and 45 degrees Celsius.
Flying in sustained winds above 12 meters per second is not recommended.

## Error Codes

Error code E-42 indicates that the motor controller has overheated; land the AD-7
immediately and allow it to cool for at least 15 minutes. Error code E-17 indicates
a GPS lock failure and the drone will refuse to enter autonomous mode until resolved.

## Warranty and Support

The AD-7 carries a 24-month limited warranty from the date of purchase. Warranty
claims and technical questions go to support@lumen-aerospace.example. Replacement
LX-900 battery packs are covered for the first 12 months only.

## Maintenance

Inspect the propellers before every flight. The recommended propeller replacement
interval is every 200 flight hours. Use only Lumen-certified propellers, part
number PR-7C, to maintain the warranty.
